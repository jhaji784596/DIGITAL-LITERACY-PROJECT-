# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes targeting students and online users. In this type of fraud, scammers trick victims into sending money using fake payment requests or phishing links. The fraud usually begins when the attacker contacts the victim through phone calls, SMS, or social media. They may pretend to be a bank representative, online seller, or customer support executive.

The scammer then sends a fake payment request and tells the victim to accept it to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim actually sends money to the fraudster. In some cases, the attacker shares a phishing link that looks like a payment page. When the victim enters their UPI PIN, the attacker gains access and transfers money.

Students and online shoppers are common targets because they frequently use UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. The consequences include financial loss, account compromise, and misuse of personal information. Many victims panic and do not report immediately, which makes recovery difficult.

UPI fraud can be prevented by understanding that money is never received by entering a PIN. Users should always verify requests, avoid unknown links, and report suspicious activity immediately.
